Despair

The very upsetting figure of Despair is painted by Giotto as a woman who hanged herself. Her hands are tense, symbolizing a violent death. The tick robe she used to hang herself is knotted to a wooden stick, which is bent by the weight of the lady. Her neck appears to be broken. This figure is the last one on the left side wall before the Last Judgment. From Hell, a demon arrives close to this vice and is pulling out the woman’s hair. Since the woman disobeyed the theological virtue of Hope, and did not appreciate God’s gift of life, she is condemned to be tortured in Hell forever.

Artist: Giotto

Dating: 1303-1305

Medium: Fresco

Dimension: 120x60cm (47,2x23,6in)

Vice Number: First

Opposite Virtue: Hope

Latin Inscription: Disperatio
