Temperance

Giotto imagines Temperance as a woman holding a sword. A rope is tied to the handle, which indicates people with good self-control never use physical force. She stands firmly and wears a long tunic. The hood that covers her head symbolizes her lack of interest in showing off. She always remains humble, and this is the reason why her actions are effective.

Artist: Giotto

Dating: 1303-1305

Medium: Fresco

Dimension:120x55cm (47,2x21,6in)

Virtue Number: Fifth

Opposite Vice: Ire

Latin Inscription: Tempantia
